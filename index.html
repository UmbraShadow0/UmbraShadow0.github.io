History

Quinta da Alegria History

Establishment (1950s):

Founded in 1953 by Manuel Silva, Quinta da Alegria began as a small-scale operation in the picturesque region of São Mamede Mountains, Portugal. Manuel started with a modest group, laying the groundwork for a legacy of poultry fighting.

Growth and Modernization (1970s - 1980s):

The fight clube expanded in the 1970s under the guidance of Manuel's children, Sofia and João Silva. Embracing technological advancements, Quinta da Alegria introduced modern coop designs and sustainable practices to meet the growing demand for exemplary fighters.

Adaptation and Resilience (1990s - 2000s):

Navigating challenges in the late 20th century, Quinta da Alegria faced economic shifts, disease outbreaks and cops searches. Manuel Silva's resilience prevailed as the fighting club diversified its hideouts and products, incorporating "Build your Chicken" to meet changing consumer preferences.

Sustainability and Innovation (2010s - Present):

Under Sofia Silva's leadership, Quinta da Alegria has become a pioneer in chicken fighting clubs. Implementing solar energy, water conservation, and waste recycling, the club ensures responsible practices and go under the radars of the cops. Cage-free, gene modifications, and supplementing-raised systems highlight Quinta da Alegria's commitment to create the strongest of chickens.

Future Vision:

Looking ahead, Quinta da Alegria continues to actively engage with the community through events and different bet payments programs. The farm envisions a future where become the number one of the fighting clubs in the world. The Silva family remains dedicated to their legacy, producing quality poultry to find the strongest of the chickens.
